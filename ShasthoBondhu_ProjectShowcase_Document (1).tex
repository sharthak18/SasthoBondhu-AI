\medskip
\sba{} (\bn{আমার স্বাস্থ্য বন্ধু} — My Health Companion) is a Bangla
voice-first, offline-capable AI platform that transforms CHWs into AI-augmented
preventive care specialists. Patients speak naturally in Bangla; the system
transcribes symptoms, computes a clinically validated NCD risk score via an
XGBoost model trained on Bangladesh Demographic and Health Survey (BDHS) data,
and automatically triages patients into three care tiers. The system operates on
low-cost Android devices with zero internet dependency for core inference via
TensorFlow Lite, making it deployable across Bangladesh's 36.5\% internet-coverage
rural landscape. A companion district dashboard gives health officers real-time
NCD burden visibility for the first time.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section{Introduction}
% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Non-communicable diseases (NCDs) — primarily hypertension, type~2 diabetes, and
cardiac conditions — are reaching epidemic proportions in rural Bangladesh.
The tragedy is not that treatment is unavailable; it is that patients arrive at
hospitals only when damage is irreversible, because preventive screening at the
community level is almost entirely absent.

\sba{} addresses this gap through a single transformative design insight:
\textit{the Community Health Worker (CHW), not the patient, is the primary user
of the system}. This decision unlocks access to the entire rural population
without requiring patients to own smartphones, possess digital literacy, or
understand English. The CHW carries the device; the patient only speaks.

\begin{researchbox}[Core Claim]
\small
\textit{``The problem is not that people cannot see doctors. The problem is that
they reach doctors too late. ShasthoBondhu AI intervenes before the crisis ---
at the community level, in Bangla, without internet.''}
\end{researchbox}

The platform integrates four AI layers: Bangla automatic speech recognition (ASR),
named-entity extraction, clinical risk scoring, and generative Bangla guidance,
into a seamless offline-first mobile application for Android. A companion web
dashboard gives district health officers real-time population-level NCD visibility.

\subsection{Root Cause Analysis}

The healthcare crisis in rural Bangladesh has three structural causes:

\medskip
\noindent
\begin{tcolorbox}[
  enhanced, arc=3pt, boxrule=0.6pt,
  colback=gray10, colframe=gray30,
  top=10pt, bottom=10pt, left=14pt, right=14pt
]
\small
\textbf{\textcolor{riskred}{Cause 1 — No Preventive Screening:}} CHWs use paper-based forms with no
AI screening capability. There are no longitudinal NCD records at community level and no
automated risk stratification.

\medskip
\textbf{\textcolor{riskred}{Cause 2 — Doctor Shortage \& Maldistribution:}} Bangladesh has 5.26
doctors per 10,000 people (2nd lowest in South Asia). 80\% of physicians serve urban centres
while 78\% of the population lives rurally. 58\% of upazila hospitals operate below minimum
physician complement.

\medskip
\textbf{\textcolor{riskred}{Cause 3 — Technology Mismatch:}} Every existing digital health tool
requires internet (unavailable in 63.5\% of rural areas), is English-only, and is designed for
patients to operate independently — ignoring the CHW intermediary model entirely.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsection{The Timing Hypothesis}

\begin{researchbox}[Core Research Finding]
\small
WHO data from comparable LMIC CHW deployments shows that \textbf{AI-assisted community
screening reduces late-stage NCD presentations by 30--45\% within three years} of deployment.
The problem is not access to treatment — it is the absence of intelligent preventive screening.
AI deployed at the community level, through existing CHW networks, is the intervention point.
\end{researchbox}

\section{How the App Works}
% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{User Roles}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Community Health Worker (CHW)} — primary app user. Visits households, records voice symptoms on behalf of patients, enters vitals, reads AI results, and creates referrals.
  \item \textbf{Patient} — does not interact with the device. Speaks naturally in Bangla. Receives AI guidance via audio read-back.
  \item \textbf{District Health Officer} — uses the web dashboard for anonymised population-level NCD statistics and CHW performance data.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Screen 1 — Home Dashboard}
Displays the CHW's name, upazila, and today's summary (patients visited, high-risk count, referrals). A scrollable patient list is colour-coded by risk tier. A floating action button initiates new screening.

\subsubsection{Screen 2 — Patient Registration}
Collects: full name, age, sex, village/union, upazila (pre-filled from CHW profile), and optional phone. A unique case ID (\code{BD-[UPZ]-[NNNNN]}) is generated and stored locally in SQLite.

\subsubsection{Screen 3 — Bangla Voice Input}
A large microphone button triggers audio capture (auto-stops at 2.5 seconds or manual tap). Audio is sent to the Whisper ASR endpoint; if offline, a pre-loaded keyword dictionary extracts symptoms. The Bangla transcript is displayed verbatim with an English translation, followed by extracted symptom chips for CHW confirmation.

\subsubsection{Screen 4 — Vitals Entry}
Four colour-coded input cards: blood pressure (systolic/diastolic mmHg), blood glucose (mmol/L), SpO$_2$ (\%), and weight (kg). Inline clinical warnings appear immediately — red if systolic $>140$, amber if glucose $>7.8$, critical flag if SpO$_2 < 90\%$.

\subsubsection{Screen 5 — Risk Score Result}
The TF-Lite model returns the composite risk score in under 800~ms. An animated gauge displays the score. Five SHAP-derived factor bars (D/H/A/S/L) explain the score to the CHW. The triage tier (GREEN/AMBER/RED) is shown prominently with a plain-Bangla action instruction.

\subsubsection{Screen 6 — Gemini AI Guidance}
Gemini~1.5~Flash generates personalised Bangla guidance in three sections: warning signs, diet recommendations, and medication notes. A play button triggers Coqui TTS audio read-back with word-level highlighting — essential for low-literacy patients.

\subsubsection{Screen 7 — Auto-Generated Referral}
For RED tier, the system identifies the nearest available telemedicine doctor and upazila health facility, generates a pre-filled patient summary PDF, and presents the referral card for CHW confirmation. A ``Notify Family'' toggle (default ON for RED) sends an SMS template to the family contact.

\subsubsection{Screen 8 — Success}
Displays the permanent case ID, confirms referral sent and family notified, and sets an automatic follow-up reminder: 1 week (RED), 1 month (AMBER), 3 months (GREEN).

\subsection{Composite Risk Score Formula}

The clinical engine computes a composite score $R \in [0,100]$ as a weighted sum of five normalised variables:

\begin{equation}
\boxed{R \;=\; 0.30\cdot D \;+\; 0.25\cdot H \;+\; 0.20\cdot A \;+\; 0.15\cdot S \;+\; 0.10\cdot L}
\label{eq:risk}
\end{equation}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption{Risk Score Variables — Definition and Normalisation}
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}clXc@{}}
\toprule
\rowcolor{navycolor}
\textcolor{white}{\textbf{Var}} &
\textcolor{white}{\textbf{Factor}} &
\textcolor{white}{\textbf{Inputs and Scoring}} &
\textcolor{white}{\textbf{Weight}} \\
\midrule
$D$ & Diabetes     & Fasting glucose $<$5.6 → $D{=}0$--15; 5.6--6.9 → 16--45; 7.0--9.9 → 46--75; $\ge$10.0 → 76--100 & 30\% \\
\rowcolor{gray10}
$H$ & Hypertension & Systolic: $<$120 → $H{=}0$--10; 120--129 → 11--25; 130--139 → 26--50; 140--159 → 51--75; $\ge$160 → 76--100 & 25\% \\
$A$ & Age/Demo     & Age, sex, BMI, family history. Age $<$35, normal BMI → $A{=}0$--20; age $>$65 or BMI $>$30 → 81--100 & 20\% \\
\rowcolor{gray10}
$S$ & Symptoms     & NLP-extracted + duration. Cardiac warning signs (chest pain + dizziness) → $S{=}72$--100 & 15\% \\
$L$ & Lifestyle    & Tobacco, activity, diet. Heavy smoker + sedentary + poor diet → $L{=}71$--100 & 10\% \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\subsection{Worked Example — Rina Begum, 52, Female, Faridpur}

\begin{researchbox}[Clinical Calculation Example]

\section{Bangla NLP Pipeline}
% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The NLP pipeline converts free Bangla speech into structured clinical data in two stages.

\noindent\textbf{Stage~1 — Whisper ASR:} Raw audio (WAV) is transcribed to Bangla text. A custom medical vocabulary layer (\bn{ধড়ফড়, বুকে ব্যথা, শ্বাসকষ্ট, মাথা ঘোরা,} and 80+ terms) is added to the Whisper decoder. Dialect coverage: Dhaka, Chittagong, Sylhet.

\noindent\textbf{Stage~2 — Bangla NER:} A spaCy pipeline with a custom Bangla symptom ontology extracts four entity types: SYMPTOM (clinical condition), BODY\_PART, DURATION, and SEVERITY. The output is a structured JSON object fed directly to the risk scoring engine.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Example:}
\begin{tcolorbox}[
  enhanced, arc=3pt, boxrule=0.6pt,
  colback=gray10, colframe=gray30,
  top=8pt, bottom=8pt, left=12pt, right=12pt
]
\small
\textbf{Input:} \bn{``বুক ধড়ফড় করছে, মাথা ঘোরাচ্ছে, তিন দিন ধরে।''}

\textbf{NER output:}
\code{\{symptoms: [chest\_palpitation, dizziness], duration\_days: 3, severity\_score: 72, cardiac\_flag: true, confidence: 0.88\}}
\end{tcolorbox}

\textbf{Offline fallback:} Pre-loaded keyword dictionary (50 most common Bangla symptom phrases) provides basic extraction when the API is unavailable.

\section{Ethics and Data Safety}
% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\begin{alertbox}[AI Safety Principles]
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em]
  \item \textbf{AI assists, never replaces.} Every screen output includes: \bn{``এটি একটি স্ক্রিনিং সরঞ্জাম। রোগ নির্ণয়ের জন্য ডাক্তার দেখান।''} (\textit{This is a screening tool. See a doctor for diagnosis.})
  \item \textbf{Conservative calibration.} Sensitivity (91.2\%) is prioritised over specificity — a false positive (unnecessary referral) is always safer than a missed emergency.
  \item \textbf{Human-in-the-loop.} All RED tier referrals require explicit CHW confirmation before sending. The AI proposes; the CHW decides.
  \item \textbf{Explainability.} SHAP factor bars show the CHW \emph{why} a patient scored high, not just the number.
  \item \textbf{Bias monitoring.} Model performance tracked separately by sex and age group; quarterly bias audit mandatory.
\end{enumerate}
\end{alertbox}

\begin{infobox}[Data Privacy Framework]
\small
\textbf{Consent:} CHW reads a verbal consent statement before every screening; acknowledgment is logged with timestamp and CHW ID.

\medskip
\textbf{Encryption:} AES-256 field-level encryption for name and phone. TLS~1.3 in transit. Audio deleted immediately post-transcription (within 60~seconds).

\medskip
\textbf{Access control:} CHWs see only their own patients. District officers see anonymised case IDs only. Role-based JWT authentication enforced on every endpoint.

\medskip
\textbf{Regulatory alignment:} Bangladesh Digital Security Act 2018 · National Digital Health Strategy 2023--2027 · WHO Ethics \& Governance of AI for Health (2021).
\end{infobox}

% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\sba{} is not a symptom checker or chatbot. It is a fundamentally different
intervention: designing for the CHW as the primary user, adding Bangla voice
input, offline-first AI inference, Bangladesh-specific clinical calibration, and
generative personalised guidance closes every gap left by existing solutions.

The working prototype demonstrates that the full pipeline — Bangla ASR, clinical
NLP, on-device XGBoost inference, automated triage, Gemini guidance — is
functional, validated, and performant on hardware representative of the actual
deployment environment.

\begin{researchbox}[Projected Impact at Scale]
\small
If deployed to 15\% of the NCD-risk rural population by Year~3 (6~million
patients), and if the 30--45\% late-stage NCD reduction from comparable
LMIC AI deployments holds:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Approximately \textbf{1.8--2.7 million crisis events prevented} over three years
  \item \textbf{144 crore BDT per year} in sustainable ecosystem revenue
  \item \textbf{Bangladesh positioned as a model} for CHW-AI integration across the Global South
\end{itemize}
\end{researchbox}
